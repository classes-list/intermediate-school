Essential Question: How can we design and build solutions to the problems around us?

📖 Unit Storyline

Students learn how engineers ask questions, define problems, and plan investigations to develop solutions. They discover how technology changes to meet the needs of people and the problems they face.

LESSON 1 Engineering Design
NGSS: 3-5-ETS1-1, 3-5-ETS1-2, 3-5-ETS1-3

🎯 Objective

Identify the steps of the engineering design process and use them to define a problem, plan a solution, and test a prototype.

Anchoring Phenomenon: How can you design and build a lightweight, non-electric listening device to use on a nature walk?

🧪 Activities

  • Exploration: What Engineers Do — students observe engineers at work and describe how they solve problems.
  • Hands-On Activity: Make and Test a Listening Device — using cups, string, tape, and paper, students design, build, and test a simple listener.
  • Fair Test — students identify variables to keep the same and one variable to change, so their test is a fair test.
  • Iterate: Test → Improve → Retest, keeping notes at each stage.
  • Looking Back to Look Forward — students compare their design to older technology (like a stethoscope) and imagine what future engineers might invent.

💡 Key Ideas

  • Engineering is the process of designing new or improved technology.
  • The Engineering Design Process has repeating steps: Ask → Imagine → Plan → Create → Test → Improve.
  • Criteria are what the solution must do; constraints are the limits (time, materials, cost).
  • Engineers use models and prototypes to test their ideas before building the real thing.
