⚙️ Engineering and Technology

Essential Question: How can we design and build solutions to the problems around us?

📖 Unit Storyline

Students learn how engineers ask questions, define problems, and plan investigations to develop solutions. They discover how technology changes to meet the needs of people and the problems they face.

LESSON 1 Ask & Notice — What Engineers Do
NGSS: 3-5-ETS1-1

🎯 Objective

Recognize what engineers do and identify problems around us that engineering can solve.

Anchoring Phenomenon: How can you design and build a lightweight, non-electric listening device to use on a nature walk?

🧪 Activities

  • Exploration: Notice & Wonder — students study photos of engineers at work and record what they notice and what they wonder.
  • Discussion: What is Engineering? — engineering is the process of designing new or improved technology to solve human problems.
  • Sort: Technology vs Nature — students categorize a set of items (pencil, tree, phone, rock) as technology or nature.
  • Journal: Real Problems — students list three problems they see in their own life that an engineer could help solve.

💡 Key Ideas

  • Engineering is the process of designing solutions to problems.
  • Technology is anything made by people to meet a need — a pencil is technology, a tree is not.
  • Engineers ask lots of questions BEFORE they build anything.

LESSON 2 Define the Problem — Criteria & Constraints
NGSS: 3-5-ETS1-1

🎯 Objective

Define a simple design problem by listing criteria for success and constraints on materials, time, or cost.

Anchoring Phenomenon: Engineers can't design anything until they know exactly what "success" means. What makes a good problem statement?

🧪 Activities

  • Class Chart: Criteria vs Constraints — build a T-chart together.
  • Practice: Read three example design problems and identify each criterion and constraint.
  • Hands-On: Write Our Own Problem — students draft a problem statement for their listening device, including at least 3 criteria and 2 constraints.
  • Peer Review — partner up and improve each other's problem statements.

💡 Key Ideas

  • Criteria = what the solution MUST DO (goals to achieve).
  • Constraints = the LIMITS on the solution (budget, size, time, materials allowed).
  • A clear problem statement helps engineers know when they're finished.

LESSON 3 Imagine & Plan — Sketch a Prototype
NGSS: 3-5-ETS1-2

🎯 Objective

Generate and compare multiple possible solutions to a problem based on how well each meets the criteria and constraints.

Anchoring Phenomenon: The first idea is rarely the best idea. How do engineers pick the best design to build?

🧪 Activities

  • Brainstorm Blitz — students sketch 3 different possible designs for their listening device in 10 minutes (no judging!).
  • Compare Grid — students score each design against the criteria and constraints on a rubric.
  • Pick the Winner — students choose one design to build and explain why in writing.
  • Detailed Blueprint — students draw a labeled diagram of their chosen design with dimensions and material list.

💡 Key Ideas

  • Engineers generate multiple possible solutions before picking one.
  • A prototype is a first version of a design used for testing.
  • A blueprint is a labeled drawing that shows exactly how the prototype will be built.

LESSON 4 Build & Test — Fair Testing
NGSS: 3-5-ETS1-2, 3-5-ETS1-3

🎯 Objective

Plan and carry out a fair test in which variables are controlled to compare how well a prototype meets the criteria.

Anchoring Phenomenon: How do we know if our listening device actually works better than a regular ear?

🧪 Activities

  • Hands-On: Build the Prototype — students use cups, cardboard tubes, tape, plastic wrap, string to construct their design.
  • Design a Fair Test — identify one variable to change (the device) and everything to keep the same (distance, sound source, tester, room).
  • Test & Record Data — students test with and without the device, recording what they heard on a data table.
  • Class Data — combine results across all groups and look for patterns.

💡 Key Ideas

  • In a fair test you change only ONE variable at a time.
  • Data is the evidence engineers use to decide whether their design worked.
  • Testing multiple times reduces mistakes.

LESSON 5 Improve & Communicate — Iteration
NGSS: 3-5-ETS1-3

🎯 Objective

Plan and carry out improvements to a design based on test data, then communicate the design and results to others.

Anchoring Phenomenon: Engineers rarely get it perfect on the first try. How do we make our design better?

🧪 Activities

  • Analyze: What Failed? — students look at their data and circle the parts of the design that didn't meet criteria.
  • Improve: Version 2 — students modify their prototype based on what they learned and test again.
  • Compare: Version 1 vs Version 2 — students record which version worked better and why.
  • Communication Project: Poster or Slideshow — students present their design journey (problem → prototype → test → improve → result) to the class.
  • Looking Back to Look Forward — compare the class devices to real technology (stethoscopes, hearing aids) and imagine what engineers will invent next.

💡 Key Ideas

  • Iteration means to improve a design based on what you learned from testing.
  • Engineers share what they learned so others can build on their work.
  • The Engineering Design Process is a cycle, not a straight line: Ask → Imagine → Plan → Create → Test → Improve → repeat.
