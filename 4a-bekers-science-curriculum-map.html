Unit 1: Engineering and Technology NGSS 3-5-ETS1-1; 3-5-ETS1-2; 3-5-ETS1-3 How can we design and build solutions to the problems around us? Students explore Students learn how engineers ask questions, define problems, and plan investigations to develop solutions. They discover how technology changes to meet the needs of people and the problems they face. Lesson 1 — Ask & Notice — What Engineers Do; Lesson 2 — Define the Problem — Criteria & Constraints; Lesson 3 — Imagine & Plan — Sketch a Prototype; Lesson 4 — Build & Test — Fair Testing; Lesson 5 — Improve & Communicate — Iteration. Key vocabulary: engineering, technology, criteria, constraints, prototype, design process, iteration, engineer. Quarter 1: 5 Weeks (8/3-9/4) Lesson 1 worksheet; Lesson 2 worksheet; Lesson 3 worksheet; Lesson 4 worksheet; Lesson 5 worksheet; Unit 1 assessment; Anchoring phenomenon reflection. HMH student activity guide; interactive lessons on class site; hands-on activities; class quiz + written short answer.
Unit 2: Plant and Animal Structure and Function NGSS 4-LS1-1; 4-LS1-2 How do the internal and external structures of plants and animals help them survive? Students explore Students explore how plant and animal structures support survival, growth, and reproduction. They develop and use models, then argue from evidence to explain how organisms' senses help them survive. Lesson 1 — Plant Structures — Roots, Stems, Leaves; Lesson 2 — Plant Reproduction — Flowers, Seeds & Pollination; Lesson 3 — Animal External Structures & Adaptations; Lesson 4 — Animal Internal Structures & Behaviors; Lesson 5 — Senses, Signals & Response. Key vocabulary: structure, function, adaptation, reproduction, sensory receptor, stimulus, response, trait. Quarters 1–2: 8 Weeks (9/8-11/13, spanning fall break) Lesson 1 worksheet; Lesson 2 worksheet; Lesson 3 worksheet; Lesson 4 worksheet; Lesson 5 worksheet; Unit 2 assessment; Anchoring phenomenon reflection. HMH student activity guide; interactive lessons on class site; hands-on activities; class quiz + written short answer.
Unit 3: Energy and Communication NGSS 4-PS3-1; 4-PS3-2; 4-PS3-3; 4-PS3-4; 4-PS4-1; 4-PS4-3 How is energy transferred, and how do we use it to send information? Students explore Students explore how waves show that energy is transferred and how energy and information travel through waves. They investigate devices — from cell phones to satellites — that enable humans to transmit information across vast distances. Lesson 1 — Forms of Energy Around Us; Lesson 2 — Energy Transfer & Transformation; Lesson 3 — Collisions & Stored Energy; Lesson 4 — Waves — Parts and Motion; Lesson 5 — Sound & Light Waves; Lesson 6 — Information Transfer. Key vocabulary: energy, transfer, transformation, kinetic, potential, wave, amplitude, wavelength. Quarters 2–3: 9 Weeks (11/17-2/12, spanning winter break) Lesson 1 worksheet; Lesson 2 worksheet; Lesson 3 worksheet; Lesson 4 worksheet; Lesson 5 worksheet; Lesson 6 worksheet; Unit 3 assessment; Anchoring phenomenon reflection. HMH student activity guide; interactive lessons on class site; hands-on activities; class quiz + written short answer.
Unit 4: Shaping Landforms NGSS 4-ESS1-1; 4-ESS2-1 How does Earth's surface change over time, and what evidence do we have? Students explore Students explore how landscapes change over time. They use models to examine the factors and processes that transform land, then construct explanations from patterns in their observations. Lesson 1 — Factors That Shape Earth's Surface; Lesson 2 — Fast and Slow Changes; Lesson 3 — Rock Layers Record Landform Changes. Key vocabulary: erosion, weathering, deposition, landform, sediment, glacier, canyon, delta. Quarters 3–4: 6 Weeks (2/16-4/9, spanning spring break) Lesson 1 worksheet; Lesson 2 worksheet; Lesson 3 worksheet; Unit 4 assessment; Anchoring phenomenon reflection. HMH student activity guide; interactive lessons on class site; hands-on activities; class quiz + written short answer.
Unit 5: Earth's Features and Resources NGSS 4-ESS2-2; 4-ESS3-1; 4-ESS3-2 Where are Earth's features, hazards, and resources located, and how do we use them wisely? Students explore Students use maps to identify patterns in where natural hazards occur, explore ways to reduce their impact, and learn how technology is changing how we use Earth's natural resources. Lesson 1 — Patterns on Earth; Lesson 2 — Natural Hazards; Lesson 3 — Earth's Resources; Lesson 4 — Nonrenewable Resources & Impact; Lesson 5 — Sustainability & Protecting Resources. Key vocabulary: natural hazard, natural resource, renewable, nonrenewable, seismograph, topographic map, sustainability. Quarter 4: 8 Weeks (4/12-6/3) Lesson 1 worksheet; Lesson 2 worksheet; Lesson 3 worksheet; Lesson 4 worksheet; Lesson 5 worksheet; Unit 5 assessment; Anchoring phenomenon reflection. HMH student activity guide; interactive lessons on class site; hands-on activities; class quiz + written short answer.
