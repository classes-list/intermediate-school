📋 Math Course Overview

This course focuses on developing number sense through multiplying by 1- and 2-digit numbers, adding/subtracting/multiplying fractions with like denominators, comparing decimals, and contrasting lines, angles, and quadrilaterals. Through partner Number Talks and collaborative problem solving, students will learn to value different mathematical strategies, listen to peers’ reasoning, and work together to find solutions.

Students will complete school-wide STAR Standardized Testing at the beginning, middle, and end of the school year to monitor their overall academic progress.

🏠 Homework Policy

As per school policy, students are expected to allocate time for a maximum of 40 minutes of homework per night. Your child’s 4th grade teachers will work together to ensure that the students receive a balanced homework regime aimed to create a positive learning environment that nurtures students’ intellectual growth. Students are expected to record assignments daily in their school planner. Homework and important reminders will also be posted on Google Classroom for students and families. Parents are expected to check the student’s planners daily, support their child in time management and follow through.

Late work will be accepted; however, submitting assignments on time is an important part of developing responsibility. Late submissions may affect a student’s participation grade.

🎓 Academic Integrity

Teachers have the responsibility to structure conditions during testing to alleviate the possibility of cheating; specify the types of collaboration that are discouraged and those that are encouraged; and teach students summarization of material and the distinction between summarizing and copying straight from the text.

Students have the responsibility to avoid situations that might contribute to cheating or plagiarizing, avoid unauthorized assistance, and use sources in the prescribed manner outlined by the teacher.

🕘 Absences & Tardiness

Students may remain at home in the case of illness, accident, quarantine, or a death in the family. The teacher will help the student make up for missing assignments after a short absence. When the absence is expected to be prolonged, families must notify the teacher so a plan can be developed for completing missing work.

Students are marked tardy after the 7:45 a.m. bell rings. Explanatory notes from parents and conferences with parents may be advisable.

🍎 Food & Drink

  • Food is prohibited in the classroom unless otherwise specified by the teacher.
  • Drink in the classroom is limited to water in a bottle with a sealed lid.
  • Sharing food is not permitted to ensure adherence to dietary restrictions.

📱 Technology

To minimize distractions and maximize learning, communication with students during the school day should be limited to emergencies. Student cell phones must remain off and in backpacks for the duration of a school day (this policy also applies to smart watches).

Violations: 1st offense — Warning; 2nd — Phone sent to office for pickup from Principal; 3rd — Phone sent to office and parent notified.
