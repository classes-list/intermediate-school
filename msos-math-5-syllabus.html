📋 Course Description

Math 5 is the bridge from elementary arithmetic to middle school math thinking. Using the enVisionMATH 2.0 program, students master decimal place value, fluently compute with whole numbers and decimals, deepen fraction understanding through addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division, and explore volume, coordinate geometry, patterns, and 2-D figures. Each topic is scaffolded with visual models, hands-on activities, small-group problem solving, focused practice, and math games. By year-end, students are ready for the abstract reasoning of Math 6 and for real-world quantitative thinking.

Textbook: enVisionMATH 2.0 · Common Core Grade 5 (Savvas / Pearson)

📚 Course Outline by Quarter — Math 5 (enVisionMATH 2.0)

Q1 — Place Value & Whole-Number / Decimal Operations

  • Topic 1: Understand Place Value (through thousandths)
  • Topic 2: Add and Subtract Decimals to Hundredths
  • Topic 3: Fluently Multiply Multi-Digit Whole Numbers
  • Topic 4: Use Models and Strategies to Multiply Decimals

Q2 — Division & Fractions with Common Denominators

  • Topic 5: Use Models and Strategies to Divide Whole Numbers
  • Topic 6: Use Models and Strategies to Divide Decimals
  • Topic 7: Use Equivalent Fractions to Add and Subtract Fractions

Q3 — Fractions, Data & Volume

  • Topic 8: Apply Understanding of Multiplication to Multiply Fractions
  • Topic 9: Apply Understanding of Division to Divide Fractions
  • Topic 10: Represent and Interpret Data (line plots, bar/line graphs)
  • Topic 11: Understand Volume Concepts (rectangular prisms)

Q4 — Measurement, Expressions, Coordinate Plane & Geometry

  • Topic 12: Convert Measurements (customary and metric)
  • Topic 13: Write and Interpret Numerical Expressions
  • Topic 14: Graph Points on the Coordinate Plane
  • Topic 15: Algebra — Analyze Patterns and Relationships
  • Topic 16: Geometric Measurement — Classify Two-Dimensional Figures

🎯 Math 5 Goals

In 5th grade, our goal is to build the numeric fluency, conceptual understanding, and problem-solving mindset students will carry into middle school. We focus on place-value depth (into thousandths), fluent decimal computation, fraction operations with meaning (not just tricks), volume as a measure, and the first taste of algebraic patterns and coordinate reasoning.

Every topic combines hands-on models, real-world contexts, and reflection so students don’t just get the answer — they can explain why the math works. By June, 5th graders leave prepared for the abstract thinking of Math 6, and confident in their voice as young mathematicians.
