Curriculum Map by Quarter

Each unit lists its standards, learning objectives, essential content, pacing, assessments, and resources.

Unit Standards Standards Reference Learning Objectives Essential Content & Skills Sequencing & Pacing Assessments Resources Used
Unit 1: Scripture — God's Story Std USCCB · Diocesan Curriculum Standards 1. Identify the two testaments of the Bible; 2. Locate a Scripture passage by book, chapter, verse; 3. Retell key stories of God's covenant. Overview of Old and New Testament; how to use a Bible; the covenants with Abraham, Moses, and David; the Gospels; parables of Jesus. Key vocabulary: Scripture, testament, covenant, gospel, parable, disciple. Quarter 1: 9 weeks (8/3–10/2) Bible passage-finding practice; unit quiz; parable retelling; prayer journal entries. The Catholic Children's Bible; Blest Are We Faith in Action Grade 4; Diocesan resources.
Unit 2: The Church & Sacraments · Advent Std USCCB · Diocesan Curriculum Standards 1. Name and describe the seven sacraments; 2. Explain the parts of the Mass; 3. Describe the four weeks of Advent and traditions of preparation. Seven sacraments (focus: Baptism, Reconciliation, Eucharist); parts of the Mass; roles at Mass; parish life. Liturgical Calendar — Advent: waiting, hope, Advent wreath, Jesse Tree. Quarter 2: 9 weeks (10/19–12/18) Sacraments quiz; Advent reflection journal; Mass parts diagram; group Advent prayer service. Blest Are We textbook; USCCB Advent resources; parish visits when possible.
Unit 3: Prayer & Moral Decision-Making · Lent Std USCCB · Diocesan Curriculum Standards 1. Practice four types of prayer; 2. Apply the Ten Commandments and Beatitudes to moral choices; 3. Describe Lenten disciplines and Holy Week. Types of prayer (adoration, contrition, thanksgiving, supplication); Ten Commandments; Beatitudes; conscience; forgiveness. Liturgical Calendar — Lent: prayer, fasting, almsgiving; Ash Wednesday; Stations of the Cross; Holy Week. Quarter 3: 9 weeks (1/7–3/12) Prayer types portfolio; Ten Commandments assessment; Stations of the Cross group presentation. Blest Are We textbook; USCCB Lent resources; FORMED for kids.
Unit 4: Discipleship & Mission Std USCCB · Diocesan Curriculum Standards 1. Explain corporal and spiritual works of mercy; 2. Describe the lives of Hawaiian saints; 3. Plan a class act of service. Corporal & spiritual works of mercy; St. Damien of Molokaʻi; St. Marianne Cope; vocations (marriage, single life, ordained, religious); being witnesses of God's love in our school community. Quarter 4: 9 weeks (3/29–6/3) Works of mercy reflection; saint biography project; class service-learning project; final oral presentation. Blest Are We textbook; Hawaiian saints biographies; parish partnerships.
