Social Studies — Grade 4

Exploring the history and geography of the five U.S. Regions and deepening our understanding of Hawaiian culture through the Hawaiiana unit.

🌟 Mary, Star of the Sea School Mission

Nurture, Unite, Inspire. We are committed to nurturing each child’s curiosity, uniting our classroom in respect and collaboration, and inspiring every student to grow in knowledge, faith, and wonder for the world around them.

🗺️ Course Units by Quarter

Q1 · Unit 1
🍂
The Northeast Region
Introduction to U.S. regions, then a deep dive into the Northeast’s geography, early colonies, and modern cities.
Topics: 5 U.S. regions overview; using maps & landform vocab; Appalachian Mountains; 13 Colonies; New England vs. Mid-Atlantic; industry & culture today.
Q2 · Unit 2
🌾
The Southeast & Midwest
Compare the Southeast’s wetlands and history with the Midwest’s plains, agriculture, and inventions.
Topics: Mississippi & Ohio Rivers; Everglades; Civil War roots; Great Plains & Great Lakes; farming, industry, transportation; regional culture.
Q3 · Unit 3
🏜️
The Southwest & West
From canyons and deserts to mountains and coastlines — the two regions that shape the American West.
Topics: Grand Canyon; Rio Grande; Native peoples of the Southwest; Rocky Mountains; Pacific coast; national parks; westward expansion.
Q4 · Unit 4
🌺
Hawaiiana
Deep dive into our home — the history, culture, values, and legacy of Hawaiʻi and its people.
Topics: Polynesian voyaging; Hawaiian ali‘i & the kingdom era; Kamehameha I; annexation and statehood; ʻōlelo Hawaiʻi vocabulary; hula, mele, and mo‘olelo.

Textbook: myWorld Interactive (Savvas / Pearson)
