Religion — Grade 4 — Course Syllabus

Mary, Star of the Sea School · Ms. Lori Glavan · SY 2026-2027

📋 Course Description

Students will deepen their understanding of Scripture, Catholic teachings, prayer, and moral decision-making while growing in their relationship with God. The Liturgical Calendar (Advent in Q2, Lent in Q3) anchors seasonal reflection and prayer alongside the content units.

Textbook: Blest Are We Second Edition: Faith in Action · The Catholic Children’s Bible

📊 Grading Policy

30%AssessmentsOral / written tests and quizzes
30%ClassworkDay-to-day practice & reflections
15%Prayer & ReflectionIn-class reflections and journals
15%Participation / HWGroup prayer, open discussion
10%Projects & Presentations

📚 Course Outline by Quarter

Q1 — Scripture: God’s Story

  • Introduction to the Bible — Old and New Testaments
  • Locating passages by book, chapter, verse
  • God’s covenant with Abraham, Moses, and David
  • The Gospels & parables of Jesus

Q2 — The Church & Sacraments · 🕯️ Advent

  • The seven sacraments (focus: Baptism, Reconciliation, Eucharist)
  • Parts of the Mass & roles at Mass
  • Liturgical Calendar — Advent: waiting, hope, and preparation for Christmas
  • Christmas season traditions

Q3 — Prayer & Moral Decision-Making · ✝️ Lent

  • Types of prayer: adoration, contrition, thanksgiving, supplication
  • The Ten Commandments and the Beatitudes
  • Conscience and moral decision-making
  • Liturgical Calendar — Lent: prayer, fasting, almsgiving; Stations of the Cross; Holy Week

Q4 — Discipleship & Mission

  • Corporal and spiritual works of mercy
  • Hawaiian saints: St. Damien and St. Marianne
  • Vocations & being a witness of God’s love
  • Class service project reflection

🏠 Homework Policy

Per school policy, students are expected to allocate up to 40 minutes of homework per night. The 4th grade teachers coordinate to balance homework across subjects. Students record assignments in their planner and check Google Classroom for reminders. Parents are expected to check planners daily and support time management.

Late work is accepted, but submitting on time is important for developing responsibility. Late submissions may affect the participation grade.

🧑‍🏫 Classroom Expectations

  • Come to class prepared and ready to learn each day.
  • Complete homework on time; communicate in advance if circumstances prevent completion.
  • Participate by asking questions, contributing ideas, and seeking clarification.
  • Treat ourselves, one another, and school property with respect and responsibility.

🎓 Academic Integrity

Teachers will structure testing to alleviate the possibility of cheating, specify allowed collaboration, and teach the difference between summarizing and copying.

Students avoid situations that contribute to cheating or plagiarizing, avoid unauthorized assistance, and use sources as directed.

🕘 Absences & Tardiness

Students may stay home for illness, accident, quarantine, or a death in the family. For extended absences, families notify the teacher so a make-up plan can be arranged. Students are tardy after the 7:45 a.m. bell.

🍎 Food & Drink

Food is prohibited unless specified by the teacher. Only sealed water bottles allowed. Sharing food is not permitted (allergies and dietary restrictions).

📱 Technology

Cell phones and smart watches must be off and in backpacks during the school day. 1st offense = warning; 2nd = phone to office; 3rd = parent notified.
