🎯 Religion Course Overview

Students will deepen their understanding of Scripture, Catholic teachings, prayer, and moral decision-making while growing in their relationship with God. Through daily prayer and reflection, we will actively live out our Schoolwide Learning Expectations by becoming Active Christians and Responsible Citizens. The Liturgical Calendar (Advent in Q2, Lent in Q3) anchors seasonal reflection and prayer alongside our content units.

Standardized testing: Students take the STAR assessment in fall, winter, and spring to track growth. English-language learners take the WIDA assessment as required.

📝 Homework Policy

As per school policy, students are expected to allocate time for a maximum of 40 minutes of homework per night (MSOS guideline: ~10 minutes per grade). Your child’s 4th grade teachers work together to ensure a balanced homework regime. Students record assignments in their planner. Homework and reminders are also posted on Google Classroom.

Late work will be accepted; however, submitting on time is important. Late submissions may affect the participation grade.

Tips for supporting homework at home:

  • Set a consistent, quiet study time and place.
  • Read directions together, then step back and let your child try.
  • Ask your child to explain the story or teaching back to you — teaching cements learning.
  • If a task takes much longer than expected, please note it and email me.

🤝 Classroom Procedures & Behavior Expectations

  • Students come to class prepared and ready to learn each day.
  • Homework should be completed on time. If circumstances prevent completion, students should communicate with the teacher in advance so an appropriate plan can be made.
  • Everyone is encouraged to participate by asking questions, contributing ideas, and seeking clarification.
  • We will treat ourselves, one another, and school property with respect and responsibility.

Positive recognition: Students earn recognition through verbal praise, positive notes home, sticker/star acknowledgments, and class-wide rewards (extra recess, class read-aloud pick, review-day celebrations).

When expectations are not met (supportive, restorative sequence):

  1. Quiet private reminder and reset.
  2. Reflection sheet / think-time in class.
  3. Loss of a small privilege and a note home.
  4. Phone call or email home; if needed, a family meeting with the principal.

The goal is always to help students re-choose kind, respectful, on-task behavior — not to punish.

🏡 How to Support Your Child at Home

  • Routines: Build a consistent homework/prayer time and a distraction-free spot.
  • Pray together as a family — mealtime and bedtime prayers are simple and powerful.
  • Attend Mass together and talk about the Gospel reading afterward.
  • Independence: Let your child pack their own backpack and check the assignment sheet.
  • Growth mindset: Praise effort and strategy (“I saw you keep trying”) more than just results.
  • Communication: Check Beehively weekly with your student.
  • Contact me early if something at home or school is affecting learning.

🎓 Academic Integrity

Teachers structure conditions during testing to alleviate the possibility of cheating; specify allowed collaboration; and teach summarization vs. copying.

Students avoid situations that might contribute to cheating or plagiarizing, avoid unauthorized assistance, and use sources in the prescribed manner.

🕘 Absences & Tardiness

Students may remain at home in the case of illness, accident, quarantine, or a death in the family. For prolonged absences, families must notify the teacher so a plan can be developed.

Students are marked tardy after the 7:45 a.m. bell. Explanatory notes from parents may be advisable.

🍎 Food & Drink

  • Food is prohibited in the classroom unless otherwise specified by the teacher.
  • Drink in the classroom is limited to water in a bottle with a sealed lid.
  • Sharing food is not permitted to ensure adherence to dietary restrictions.

📱 Technology

To minimize distractions and maximize learning, communication with students during the school day should be limited to emergencies. Student cell phones must remain off and in backpacks (this policy also applies to smart watches).

Violations: 1st — Warning; 2nd — Phone sent to office for pickup from Principal; 3rd — Phone sent to office and parent notified.
