Curriculum Map by Quarter

Each unit lists its standards, learning objectives, essential content, pacing, assessments, and resources.

Unit Standards Standards Reference Learning Objectives Essential Content & Skills Sequencing & Pacing Assessments Resources Used
Unit 1: U.S. Regions Overview + Northeast Region Std C3 D2.Geo.1-4; D2.His.1-3 1. Identify the five U.S. regions on a map; 2. Describe the landforms, climate, and resources of the Northeast; 3. Retell the story of the 13 Colonies. 5 U.S. regions overview; map skills; Appalachian Mountains; Atlantic coast; 13 Colonies; New England vs. Mid-Atlantic culture; industry & modern cities. Quarter 1: 9 weeks (8/3–10/2) Map skills quiz; Northeast region project; 13 Colonies timeline; unit test. myWorld Interactive; National Geographic Kids; iCivics; regional maps & readings.
Unit 2: Southeast & Midwest Regions Std C3 D2.Geo.2, 5-9; D2.His.4-6 1. Compare the Southeast and Midwest by geography and economy; 2. Explain the importance of the Mississippi River system; 3. Identify agricultural and industrial contributions of each region. Southeast: Mississippi & Ohio Rivers; Everglades; Civil War roots (age-appropriate); Midwest: Great Plains, Great Lakes; farming, invention, transportation. Regional cultures. Quarter 2: 9 weeks (10/19–12/18) Regional-comparison foldable; weekly quizzes; Midwest inventor spotlight; unit test. myWorld Interactive; regional videos; Ducksters social studies articles.
Unit 3: Southwest & West Regions Std C3 D2.Geo.2, 5-9; D2.His.4-6 1. Describe the physical geography of the Southwest and West; 2. Explain Spanish colonial influence in the Southwest; 3. Describe westward expansion and the modern West. Southwest: Grand Canyon, Rio Grande, deserts, Native peoples; Spanish colonial history; missions. West: Rocky Mountains, Pacific Coast, national parks, westward expansion. Quarter 3: 9 weeks (1/7–3/12) National park research project; westward expansion timeline; weekly quizzes; unit test. myWorld Interactive; National Park Service Kids; virtual field trips.
Unit 4: Hawaiiana Std Hawaiʻi State Studies Standards; C3 D2.His.1-14 1. Trace the settlement of Hawaiʻi by Polynesian voyagers; 2. Explain the role of Kamehameha I in unifying the islands; 3. Describe key moments from the kingdom era to statehood. Polynesian voyaging; Hawaiian aliʻi and kapu system; Kamehameha I; overthrow of the Kingdom; annexation; statehood (1959); ʻōlelo Hawaiʻi vocabulary; hula, mele, moʻolelo. Quarter 4: 9 weeks (3/29–6/3) Hawaiiana research project; ʻIolani Palace field-trip journal (3/5); moʻolelo retell; final unit test. myWorld Interactive; hawaiihistory.org; Bishop Museum resources; ʻIolani Palace visit.
