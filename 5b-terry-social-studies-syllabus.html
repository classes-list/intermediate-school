👋 Welcome to Grade 5

Welcome to Grade 5. This syllabus is intended for you, the student, to develop a greater understanding of what we will cover, what is expected of you, and what you can expect from me.

Living our NUI Mission: A supportive environment is essential for student growth and achievement. Students are to act respectfully towards other students and to respond to each other’s ideas and work with thoughtful comments, positive encouragement, and constructive feedback. Students are expected to be attentive, to work diligently during class, and to follow all classroom rules and directions.

🌍 Social Studies Course Overview

Students will be learning about the history of people in North America beginning with the first indigenous tribes and ending with Manifest Destiny and the expansion of the United States across the continent. Students will learn about important themes throughout our history, engage in research and analysis of historical events, and participate in cross-curricular projects using other subjects such as English and Art.

Textbook: MyWorld Interactive (Social Studies)

📊 Grading Note for Parents

You will have access to my electronic gradebook via your Beehively account, which will be the primary method of communicating your child’s academic performance. It will be assumed that you have seen all grades and missing assignments that are entered in Beehively.

📝 Homework Policy

Homework that is given will not be for students to explore new concepts. Homework will strictly be to reinforce concepts that have already been taught in class, including research for projects. It is used as more of a “completion” grade. Your children’s teachers will work together to ensure that the students receive a balanced homework regime aimed at creating a positive learning environment that nurtures students’ intellectual growth.

💬 Communication

The best way to communicate with a teacher is by email. Students will be sent home with a white envelope on Tuesdays that has updated work and various other documents regarding events at school.

Updates will also be sent out via Beehively, our electronic communication and grading portal. Announcements posted to Beehively will be sent directly to your email as well as important upcoming assignments and events.

⏱️ Late Work & Extra Credit

  • All late work must be turned in within one week of when it was assigned.
  • Students will receive 70% on late work turned in.
  • Extra credit is not offered. However, during the last week of each quarter, students may turn in any missing or incomplete work for partial credit (60%).
  • 10% will be taken off of project grades for late submissions.
