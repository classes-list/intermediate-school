🌱 Plant and Animal Structure and Function

Essential Question: How do the internal and external structures of plants and animals help them survive?

📖 Unit Storyline

Students explore how plant and animal structures support survival, growth, and reproduction. They develop and use models, then argue from evidence to explain how organisms' senses help them survive.

LESSON 1 Plant Parts and How They Function
NGSS: 4-LS1-1

🎯 Objective

Investigate how plant structures (roots, stems, leaves, flowers) support survival, growth, and reproduction.

Anchoring Phenomenon: Why does an apple tree look different in each season, and how do its structures change?

🧪 Activities

  • Anchoring Phenomenon: Sugaring Season — students observe how a maple tree changes in early spring.
  • Hands-On: Flower Power — students dissect a flower to identify petals, stamen, pistil, and reason about pollination.
  • Investigation: Plant Structures Have Special Functions — students test how a stem transports water using celery and colored water.
  • Modeling Activity: How Plants Change — students draw and label plant parts and connect each structure to its function.

💡 Key Ideas

  • Roots anchor the plant and absorb water and nutrients from soil.
  • Stems transport water and nutrients between roots and leaves, and support the plant.
  • Leaves collect sunlight and make food through photosynthesis.
  • Flowers and fruits are structures for reproduction — producing seeds for new plants.

LESSON 2 Animal Parts and How They Function
NGSS: 4-LS1-1

🎯 Objective

Explore how animal body structures support survival, growth, behavior, and reproduction.

Anchoring Phenomenon: A sea star can survive being torn in half. How do its body structures help it?

🧪 Activities

  • Anchoring Phenomenon: Sea Star Survival — students observe how a sea star's regeneration works.
  • Investigation: Dinner Is Served — students match different animal mouth structures to the foods they eat.
  • Modeling: Courtship Displays — students explain how peacock feathers, birdsong, and antlers help reproduction.
  • Compare: Body Building — students compare skeletons of different animals and how each structure helps survival.
  • Diagram: Inside Out — students label the digestive system of a common animal.

💡 Key Ideas

  • External structures like fur, feathers, and scales protect animals and help control body temperature.
  • Internal structures like the skeleton, muscles, heart, and lungs keep the animal alive.
  • Animal structures like mouths and teeth match what the animal eats.
  • Some structures help animals reproduce or defend themselves.

LESSON 3 How Senses Work
NGSS: 4-LS1-2

🎯 Objective

Use a model to describe how animals sense the environment, process information, and respond.

Anchoring Phenomenon: How does an owl find a mouse in complete darkness?

🧪 Activities

  • Hands-On: Touch Test — students test which parts of the body have the most sensitive touch receptors.
  • Investigation: No Smell, No Taste, No See — students hold their nose while tasting jelly beans and describe what changes.
  • Modeling: Nerves and Receptors — students draw the pathway from stimulus to sensory receptor to brain to response.
  • Compare: Sights and Sounds — students compare animal senses (bat echolocation, dog smell, eagle vision).

💡 Key Ideas

  • Sensory receptors take in information from the environment (light, sound, touch, smell, taste).
  • The brain processes that information and decides what to do.
  • The body responds — usually by moving or changing behavior.
  • Different animals have different senses that fit where they live (bats echolocate, snakes see heat, dogs smell far).
